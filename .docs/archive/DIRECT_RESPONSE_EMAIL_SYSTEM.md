## üß† Direct Response Principles Applied

### **1. Problem-Agitate-Solution (PAS)**

**Structure:**
```
Problem: You're struggling with {{issue}}
Agitate: You've tried {{things}} but still stuck
Solution: Here's {{product}} - the real answer
```

**Example in Templates:**
- Course Pain/Agitate template
- Sample Pack Scarcity (implied problem)

### **2. Scarcity & Urgency**

**Tactics:**
- Unit counts ("{{unitsLeft}} left")
- Time limits ("{{hoursLeft}} hours")
- Spot limits ("{{spotsLeft}} spots")
- Deadline enforcement
- No restock messaging

**Example:** "Once these {{units}} are gone, pulling it down. No restock."

### **3. Social Proof**

**Types:**
- Sales velocity ("{{soldCount}} sold in {{timeframe}}")
- Current enrollment ("{{studentsEnrolled}} enrolled")
- Testimonials (multiple P.S. lines)
- Results (before/after audio)
- Authority markers

### **4. Value Stacking**

**Structure:**
```
Product 1: {{value1}} ({{price1}})
Product 2: {{value2}} ({{price2}})
Bonus 1: {{value3}}
Bonus 2: {{value4}}
‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ
TOTAL: {{total}}
YOU PAY: {{discount}}
SAVE: {{savings}} ({{percent}}%)
```

### **5. Risk Reversal**

**Guarantees:**
- 30-day money-back
- "If not improving after session 1, full refund"
- "You risk nothing"
- "No questions asked"

### **6. Multiple CTAs**

**Pattern:**
- Primary CTA (button/link)
- Secondary CTA in P.S.
- Urgency reinforcement
- Alternative offer in P.P.S.
